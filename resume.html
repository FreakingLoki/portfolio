Major Responsibilities

  • Unload returned product from tractor trailers daily at a fast pace.
  • Ensure the emptied truck is ready for loading for the next delivery by organizing load bars, straps, temperature bulkheads, and pallets.
  • Check in returned product for quality issues including proper temperature, damage, or spoilage.
  • Check and record whether or not items match what was order using item numbers and descriptions.
  • Check that the reason for return matches what the driver, account manager, or customer reported.
  • Ensure the product will be returned to the correct location within the warehouse.
  • Coordinate with other inventory control personnel regarding recalls, hidden quality issues, or count discrepancies.
  • Perform inventory counts and adjustments.
  • Assist the Credits Coordinator by approving or denying credit to customers' accounts.
  • Enter credits into the computer system to approve or deny credit.
  • Watch for opportunities to reduce shrink.
  • Work in a safe and respectful manner in a busy area of the warehouse with limited space and time.
  • Maintain a clean and safe work area.

Details

I began my professional career here after leaving college in 2014. I started on the night shift as an Order Selector. Accuracy was the most important aspect of this work, but other challenges included working extremely long hours and working at a very fast pace for the entirety of those long shifts. Luckily my background in long distance running taught me a lot about setting a fast pace.


In the fall of 2016 I interveiwed for a new position within the company where I would be able to get away from being a nocturnal being. I took a pay cut, but I was willing to make that trade. The job title I was offered (and still have) was Driver Check-In Coordinator. This job has changed a lot since I took it over. In the begining I was only responsible for checking returned product for quality and ensuring the item we were restocking in our inventory database matched the actual item being returned. However in 2019 there were many changes made for the sake of Covid-19 safety. One of those meant I had to limit contact with truck drivers. I'm now responsible for offloading the products and empty pallets from the drivers' trailers when they return to the warehosue at the end of their run each day. I'm back to working at a very fast pace while ensuring I stay accurate with my quality and product checks. I have training in HAACP procedures as well as some familiarity with DOT regulations and more knowledge about food safety than my wife cares to hear about.
