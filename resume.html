Attributes

My current job requires me to be able to distinguish between thousands of products using mostly a six-digit product number system and a short, abbreviated description. I also need to accurately identify expiration dates and signs of spoilage or temperature abuse. Programming has a lot of similar problems. One typo can break an entire application.

It is the little things that will make or break your project. Small design choices can mean the difference between a website that looks like it was built in the early myspace era or something as modern as Google's Material Design. I'm no art student, but I do try my best to look for inspiration before I start designing the look of something. I spent a solid hour trying to pick between fonts for this website.

I spent my high school and junior high years running long distance in track and cross country. Set your pace and stick to it. I have set times of day that I work on certain things. I get up and make the coffee every morning for my wife and I, then I sit down for three hours to practice. I stay focused by putting on quiet, familiar music (so that I'm not distracted by deep diving into a new artist) and just plug away at whatever I need to work on until it is time for breakfast. If I didn't have to work for a living I would be in front of this screen all day and I would still enjoy every minute of it.

I wouldn't want to use a mediocre product, so why would I want to put one out there for someone else to use? I do a lot of research before buying a new tool or product for myself and my family. I work hard for my money so I expect the person accepting my money for their product would work just as hard to ensure a good experience. I do the same every day at work. I wouldn't want to slack off and let someone buy a case of spoiled produce. High quality work begets high quality relationships with your customers and your co-workers.

I show up every day. I do my work in the same way every day. I practice coding every week on the same days for the same amount of time. I get my household chores done in the same manner every day. I like to be consistent because I like people that I know to know that they can rely on me.

I hate being the last one to show up to something. I went to a concert in Boston several years ago and my friend and I got there very very early to ensure that we would get in quickly even though the show had assigned seating. I make sure I get to family functions, work, and any other appointments I may have 10-15 minutes early. Google Maps is good, but traffic can change in a matter of minutes. I don't mind waiting for someone else, because I understand that life happens. I have a wife and a daughter, I know that not everything can be done on time, and sometimes all of us forget things. I like to use alarms on my mobile phone and I use my calender for a lot of things that someone else might not.
