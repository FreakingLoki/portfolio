About Me

I'm an aspiring full stack web developer from central Maine. I spent some time enrolled at Norwich University studying Civil Engineering which turned out to be the wrong major for me. I left Norwich in 2014. When I left college I got a job at Performance Food Group as a night shift order selector. From there I worked my way up to the role of Driver Check-In Coordinator. This is an inventory control and quality control focued position which ensures fair reimbursment to customers and protects the company from unnecessary losses while keeping accurate inventory counts. I was inspired to give programming and coding a try begining in 2022 when my wife decided to go back to school. I've always had interest in computer hardware and software. I've built and rebuilt PCs and laptops. When I was in middle school I tried learning Visual Basic, but I could not wrap my head around it back then. It didn't help that every time I sat down with the intent to learn I would get distracted by video games on my old PC.

I have several hobbies, but I have trouble finding time to dedicate to them. Being a parent, homeowner, full time employee, and a student take up most of my free time. I still manage to find time to dedicate to playing video games, woodworking, and spending time outdoors with my family. I attempted to learn how to play guitar several years ago, and I learned some chords before deciding to stick to the drums. I've been playing the drums (only as a hobbiest, never in a group) for several years. I've been playing video games for my entire life, starting on the PC of course with games like Wolfenstein, Unreal Tournament, and Command and Conquer. As a new homeowner I decided to take up woodworking, hoping to learn some DIY skills and save some money on any repairs we may need. My first big woodworking project was a firewood storage structure. I think my next will be something smaller, maybe a piece of furniture.
